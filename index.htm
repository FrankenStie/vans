Vans Off The Wall

Off the Wall, Since '66

Vans off the wall is an American manufacturer of skateboarding shoes, apparel, and other related products. The company was founded on March 16, 1966, by Paul Van Doren, Gordon C. Lee, James Van Doren, and Serge D'Elia in Anaheim, California. Initially, the company sold shoes directly to the public, displaying them in their store and manufacturing them on the premises.

Vans gained popularity among skateboarders in the 1970s due to the rugged make-up and sticky sole of their shoes, which made them ideal for skateboarding. The company's shoes became iconic in the skateboarding community and were eventually featured in the 1982 movie "Fast Times at Ridgemont High," further boosting their popularity.

In the 1980s, Vans expanded its product line to include various styles of shoes, such as slip-ons, high-tops, and sandals. The company also began sponsoring skateboarders, surfers, Bikers, BMX and hosting events, further cementing its place in extreme sports culture.

Over the years, Vans has continued to innovate and expand its product offerings, including collaborations with artists, musicians, and other brands. The company has also expanded its reach globally, with stores and distribution in various countries around the world.

Today, Vans is not only a well-known brand in skateboarding but also in youth culture and fashion. The brand is known for its iconic shoes, apparel, and accessories, as well as its commitment to creativity, individuality, and self-expression.
